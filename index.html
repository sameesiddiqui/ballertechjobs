Tech Company Hiring Process

Large tech companies like Google, Facebook, and Apple prioritize a candidates ability to solve complex problems, their passion for the company's mission, and cultural fit. During each interview, your personality, problem solving, and interest in the company will be evaluated.

Companies will typically issue 1-2 technical phone screens at the beginning of the application process. These screens will generally consist of a data structures or algorithms question which you will have 45 minutes to solve.

Following the phone screens, candidates will be invited onsite for a day of interviewing. This process will typically be 4-5 more challenging coding questions, which you will be expected to solve on a whiteboard in about 1 hour. These questions are once again heavily focused on testing an applicants problem solving and their knowledge of data structures and algorithms.

There will generally also be 1 non-technical interview. Be prepared to answer questions about your background, how you work in a team, and challenges that you have faced in the past.
